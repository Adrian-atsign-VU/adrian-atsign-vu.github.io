Step 1: Password Safety

  • Use a Secure Password Manager (Offline Preferred). You can download and install password managers like KeePass.
  • Activate Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) for added security. Go to your account settings and enable 2FA. Many services offer this feature under the "Security" or "Privacy" section.
  • Choose Strong and Unique Passwords. Use a combination of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using easily guessable passwords like "password123".
  • Regularly Update Your Passwords. Visit your account settings periodically and update your passwords. Set reminders to change them every few months.

Step 2: Network Safety

  • Enable Encryption (WPA2 or WPA3) for your Wi-Fi network. Access your router settings and look for the "Wireless" or "Security" tab. Choose WPA2 or WPA3 from the encryption options.
  • Modify Default SSID and Password for your network. Navigate to the "Wireless" or "Network" settings in your router's configuration page. Change the default SSID and password to something unique.
  • Activate Firewalls and Use Secure Protocols like HTTPS. Open your device settings and navigate to the "Security" or "Privacy" section. Turn on the firewall and ensure that HTTPS is enabled in your browser settings.

Step 3: Secure Internet Browsing

  • Employ ad and pop-up blockers or anti-ad add-ons. Install browser extensions like uBlock Origin or Privacy Badger.
  • Choose privacy-focused web browsers like Brave or Firefox.
  • Use Secure and Privacy-Oriented Search Engines like DuckDuckGo or Brave Search.

Step 4: Email Security

  • Verify sender legitimacy before opening attachments or clicking links. Look for suspicious signs like unfamiliar senders or unexpected requests for personal information.
  • Only enter sensitive information on HTTPS sites or trusted platforms. Ensure that the website URL starts with "https://" and has a padlock icon in the address bar.

Step 5: Data Protection

  • Regularly back up important data offline and encrypt backup files. Use backup software and store backups on external hard drives or USB flash drives.
  • Review and adjust social media privacy settings to limit personal information exposure. Go to your account settings on social media platforms and adjust privacy settings to restrict who can view your posts and personal information.

Step 6: Phishing Awareness

  • Be cautious of suspicious emails or websites requesting sensitive information. Check email sender addresses carefully for misspellings or unusual domains.
