Password Safety Tips

  • Use a Secure Password Manager (Offline Is Better) - Choose a Strong Master Password.
  • Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA).
  • Secure Your Devices - Strong Phone Password (Avoid using FaceID or biometric authentication methods that may be employed without your consent or against your will.).
  • Do Not Use Maccas Wifi lol (Any Free and Open Network Does not Encrypted traffic).

Network Safety Tips

  • Enable Encryption (WPA2 or WPA3 encryption).
  • Change Default SSID And Password (Network/Wifi Name & Password).
  • Enable Firewalls.
  • Use Secure Protocols (HTTPS etc).
  • Ensure secure internet browsing by using blockers or anti-ad and pop-up add-ons to be safe in your online interactions.

Backup Data

Regularly back up important data to prevent loss in case of hardware failure or cyber attacks.

  • Store backups in multiple locations, including (and in my opinion a must) offline storage, to prevent data loss.
  • Encrypt backup files to protect sensitive information.
  • Test your backup systems regularly, also try to avoid online clouds.

Social Media Privacy Settings

Review and adjust privacy settings on social media platforms to control the visibility of your personal information.

  • Limit the amount of personal information you share on social media profiles!
  • Change accounts to private (Find Out How).
  • Avoid accepting friend requests or connections from unknown individuals.

Limit Personal Information

Avoid oversharing personal information online, and be cautious about the information you reveal on social media.

  • Think twice before sharing sensitive information such as your address, phone number, or birthdate.. hell even shoe size.
  • Avoid posting details about upcoming vacations or extended periods away from home.
  • Consider using privacy controls to restrict access to personal posts and photos. (Like staying private).

Phishing Awareness

Stop phishing attempts; verify the legitimacy of websites and emails, especially those requesting sensitive information.

  • Check for misspellings or unusual email addresses in suspicious emails, e.g. different language or fonts.
  • Avoid clicking on links or downloading attachments from unknown or unsolicited sources.
  • When in doubt, contact the people directly through official channels to verify requests for personal or financial information. (btw your bank or microsoft WILL NOT call you for your passwords).

Multiple Authentication Layers

Enable multi-factor authentication whenever possible for an extra layer of security.

  • Use a combination of password, biometric (if you have to..), or token-based authentication methods for added security.
  • Review and update authentication settings
  • Avoid using easily guessable passwords and consider using a password manager to securely store and manage login credentials.
